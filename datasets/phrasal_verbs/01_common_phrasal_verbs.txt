# Phrasal Verbs: Common Phrasal Verbs (B1-B2)

1. What does "give up" mean?
a) To continue trying
b) To stop trying or quit
c) To give something to someone
d) To stand up

2. "Look after" means:
a) To search for
b) To take care of
c) To look behind
d) To ignore

3. What does "put off" mean?
a) To turn off
b) To postpone
c) To put down
d) To dress

4. "Break down" can mean:
a) To repair something
b) To stop working (machine) or lose emotional control
c) To break into pieces
d) To exercise

5. What does "turn up" mean?
a) To arrive, especially unexpectedly
b) To turn around
c) To decrease volume
d) To reject

6. "Get along (with)" means:
a) To move forward
b) To have a good relationship
c) To understand
d) To receive

7. What does "look forward to" mean?
a) To look ahead
b) To anticipate with pleasure
c) To remember
d) To ignore

8. "Call off" means:
a) To phone someone
b) To cancel
c) To shout
d) To name

9. What does "find out" mean?
a) To lose something
b) To discover or learn
c) To go outside
d) To search

10. "Set up" means:
a) To sit down
b) To establish or arrange
c) To destroy
d) To wake up

11. What does "carry on" mean?
a) To lift something
b) To continue
c) To stop
d) To bring

12. "Run out of" means:
a) To go running
b) To have no more of something
c) To escape
d) To produce

13. What does "come across" mean?
a) To cross a road
b) To find by chance
c) To travel
d) To understand

14. "Pick up" can mean:
a) To drop
b) To collect someone/something or learn casually
c) To choose carefully
d) To ignore

15. What does "work out" mean?
a) To stop working
b) To exercise or solve/calculate
c) To go outside for work
d) To fail

16. "Bring up" means:
a) To take down
b) To raise (a child) or mention (a topic)
c) To carry
d) To lower

17. What does "take off" mean?
a) To put on clothes
b) To remove or (plane) leave the ground
c) To land
d) To take a photo

18. "Go through" means:
a) To avoid
b) To experience or examine carefully
c) To pass by
d) To finish quickly

19. What does "figure out" mean?
a) To count numbers
b) To understand or solve
c) To draw a figure
d) To exercise

20. "Look up" can mean:
a) To look at the sky
b) To search for information (in a book/online)
c) To admire
d) To ignore
