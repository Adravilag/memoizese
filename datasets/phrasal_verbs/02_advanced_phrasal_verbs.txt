# Phrasal Verbs: Advanced Phrasal Verbs (B2-C1)

1. What does "phase out" mean?
a) To start quickly
b) To gradually stop using or doing something
c) To lose consciousness
d) To phase in

2. "Crack down on" means:
a) To break something
b) To take severe measures against
c) To laugh at
d) To crash into

3. What does "wind up" mean?
a) To start a clock
b) To end up in a situation / to annoy someone
c) To open a window
d) To relax

4. "Fend off" means:
a) To defend a friend
b) To resist or ward off an attack
c) To offend someone
d) To surrender

5. What does "dwell on" mean?
a) To live somewhere
b) To think or talk about something too much
c) To build a house
d) To move quickly

6. "Bear with" means:
a) To carry a bear
b) To be patient with
c) To fight against
d) To give birth

7. What does "iron out" mean?
a) To remove wrinkles from clothes
b) To resolve difficulties or problems
c) To exercise
d) To go outside

8. "Play down" means:
a) To act in a play
b) To minimize the importance of something
c) To play music quietly
d) To lose a game

9. What does "branch out" mean?
a) To climb a tree
b) To expand into new areas or activities
c) To cut branches
d) To leave a company

10. "Whittle down" means:
a) To paint white
b) To gradually reduce in size or number
c) To carve wood
d) To clean something

11. What does "lash out" mean?
a) To tie something
b) To suddenly attack verbally or physically
c) To leave quickly
d) To close eyes

12. "Reel in" means:
a) To feel dizzy
b) To attract or win over gradually
c) To release
d) To spin around

13. What does "factor in" mean?
a) To multiply
b) To include as a relevant element
c) To work in a factory
d) To divide

14. "Taper off" means:
a) To record something
b) To gradually decrease or diminish
c) To wrap with tape
d) To increase steadily

15. What does "home in on" mean?
a) To go home
b) To focus attention on / move towards
c) To stay inside
d) To decorate

16. "Mull over" means:
a) To heat wine
b) To think carefully about
c) To speak quietly
d) To mix together

17. What does "ramp up" mean?
a) To build a ramp
b) To increase or intensify
c) To slow down
d) To drive up

18. "Pan out" means:
a) To use a pan
b) To develop or turn out (well or badly)
c) To photograph
d) To spread out

19. What does "flesh out" mean?
a) To gain weight
b) To add more details to something
c) To remove skin
d) To exercise

20. "Gloss over" means:
a) To add shine
b) To treat something as unimportant or hide problems
c) To explain clearly
d) To paint
