# Grammar: Conditionals Complete (B1-C1)

1. Zero conditional is used for:
a) Imaginary situations
b) General truths and facts
c) Past regrets
d) Future predictions

2. "If you ___ water to 100°C, it boils." (Zero)
a) will heat
b) heat
c) heated
d) would heat

3. First conditional is used for:
a) Impossible situations
b) Real/possible future situations
c) Past regrets
d) General facts

4. "If it ___ tomorrow, we'll cancel the picnic." (First)
a) rains
b) will rain
c) rained
d) would rain

5. Second conditional is used for:
a) Certain future events
b) Hypothetical/unlikely present or future situations
c) Past facts
d) Scientific truths

6. "If I ___ rich, I would travel the world." (Second)
a) am
b) were
c) will be
d) would be

7. Third conditional is used for:
a) Future possibilities
b) Present facts
c) Hypothetical past situations (regrets)
d) General truths

8. "If I had studied harder, I ___ the exam." (Third)
a) passed
b) would pass
c) would have passed
d) will pass

9. Which sentence is a first conditional?
a) If I were you, I would accept.
b) If you heat ice, it melts.
c) If she calls, I'll tell her.
d) If I had known, I would have come.

10. Complete: "If he ___ earlier, he wouldn't have missed the train." (Third)
a) left
b) leaves
c) had left
d) would leave

11. Mixed conditional (past → present): "If I ___ the job, I ___ happier now."
a) got / would be
b) had got / would be
c) get / will be
d) would get / am

12. "Unless you hurry, you ___ late." (Unless = If not)
a) are
b) will be
c) would be
d) were

13. "I wish I ___ speak French." (Present wish)
a) can
b) could
c) will
d) would

14. "___ I were younger, I would learn to play the piano."
a) Unless
b) If
c) When
d) Although

15. "If only I ___ to the party last night!" (Past regret)
a) went
b) go
c) had gone
d) would go

16. Which is grammatically CORRECT?
a) If I will see him, I tell him.
b) If I see him, I will tell him.
c) If I saw him, I will told him.
d) If I seen him, I would tell.

17. "What ___ you do if you won the lottery?" (Second)
a) will
b) would
c) did
d) do

18. "If she ___ the truth, she wouldn't have lied."
a) knows
b) knew
c) had known
d) would know

19. "Provided that you ___ the rules, you can join." (= If)
a) follow
b) followed
c) will follow
d) would follow

20. "If I ___ you, I wouldn't do that." (Advice)
a) am
b) were
c) was
d) Both B and C are acceptable
